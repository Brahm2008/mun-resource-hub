Committees & Roles

A practical overview of committee formats and delegate roles commonly seen across Indian school and student MUN conferences.

Why Committee Formats Differ

In Indian MUNs, not every conference follows the same structure. Some focus on classic UN committees, while others include Indian parliamentary simulations, crisis committees or special agenda formats. This page highlights the committee types students commonly encounter, but the exact style and Rules of Procedure will always depend on the conference handbook.

Conventional UN-Style Committees

UNGA / DISEC

General Assembly committees are common entry points for beginners. They are usually structured, topic-focused and easier to understand than faster-paced formats.

UNSC

The Security Council is one of the most popular committees in Indian MUNs. It often involves sharper debate, more urgency and a stronger focus on international security issues.

ECOSOC / UNHRC / WHO

These committees often focus on development, human rights, public health and social policy. They are common in school conferences and can be approachable for prepared beginners.

Unconventional Indian Committees

AIPPM / Indian Parliament

The All India Political Parties Meet (AIPPM) or Indian Parliament simulations are extremely common in India. These committees are often more political, bit fast-paced and require stronger understanding of Indian affairs. But due to the use unconventional rules of procedure and native languages, these committees are usually fun.

Lok Sabha / Rajya Sabha Simulations

Some conferences run parliamentary formats inspired by Indian legislative debate. These may differ significantly from standard UN-style MUN procedure and often involve more direct confrontation and floor dynamics.

International Press (IP)

International Press is a popular side format in many Indian MUNs. Instead of representing a country, participants report on committee proceedings, interview delegates and produce articles or coverage.

Crisis Committees

Crisis committees are dynamic and unpredictable. Delegates react to rapidly changing updates and often make decisions in a much faster and more strategic environment than regular committees.

ICJ / Legal Committees

International Court of Justice and legal simulations are also seen in some conferences. These require structured argumentation, legal reasoning and careful interpretation of facts and procedure.

Specialized or Fictional Agendas

Many Indian MUNs also experiment with unique committees, historical simulations or custom crisis formats. These can be exciting, but they often require delegates to adapt quickly to special rules.

Roles You May See in a Conference

Participant Roles

  • Delegate
  • Reporter / Journalist (in IP)
  • Cabinet Member / Minister (in some simulations)
  • Judge / Counsel (in legal or ICJ formats)

Organising & Committee Roles

  • Executive Board / Dais
  • Chairperson
  • Vice-Chair / Co-Chair
  • Rapporteur (in some formats)
  • Secretariat (conference leadership)

Best Committee Types for Beginners

  • Best starting point: UNGA-style committees, DISEC, or structured social/economic committees.
  • Good next step: UNHRC, WHO, or ECOSOC-type committees with clear agendas.
  • Try after some experience: UNSC, crisis, or high-pressure political committees.
  • Be cautious as a first-timer: AIPPM or Indian Parliament can be exciting, but they are often faster, more political and harder to control if you are brand new.
  • Having said all that, you can always go for any committee whose agenda excites you even as a first time delegate. Committee preferences do vary from delegate to delegate and MUNs are always more about experience than competition.
