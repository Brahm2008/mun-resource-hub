Opening Speech Practice

Prompt 1

You are representing Japan in a committee discussing maritime security in the Indo-Pacific. Draft a 45-second opening speech with one concern, one principle, and one solution.

Prompt 2

You are representing Brazil in a committee discussing climate finance and sustainable development. Prepare a concise opening speech focused on equity and implementation.

Prompt 3

You are representing India in a committee discussing cross-border terrorism. Deliver a firm but diplomatic opening statement with a clear call for international cooperation.

Common Motions to Practise

Frequent Motions

  • Motion to open the General Speakers’ List
  • Motion for a Moderated Caucus
  • Motion for an Unmoderated Caucus
  • Motion to introduce a draft resolution
  • Motion to move into voting procedure

How to Practise Them

  • Say them aloud until they feel natural
  • Keep wording short and confident
  • Know when each motion makes strategic sense
  • Match your motion to the flow of committee
  • Practise both formal and natural delivery styles

What Would You Say Next?

Scenario: Another delegate says your proposed solution is unrealistic because it requires more international funding than many countries can commit. How would you respond in a short, diplomatic, committee-appropriate way?

  • Acknowledge the concern instead of reacting defensively.
  • Reframe your solution as phased or scalable.
  • Show flexibility while protecting your core point.
  • Keep the tone diplomatic, not personal.

Quick Debate Drills

30-Second Drill

Pick any global issue and explain your country’s stance in 30 seconds without reading from a script.

Rebuttal Drill

Listen to a strong opposing argument and prepare a respectful rebuttal in 2 clear points.

Bloc-Building Drill

Identify 3 likely allies and explain why they may support your draft or working paper.

Clause Drill

Write one preambulatory clause and one operative clause for the same agenda in under 3 minutes.
